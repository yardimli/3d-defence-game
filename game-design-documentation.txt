## Game Design Document: System Apocalypse - Urban Defender

### 1. Game Overview

**1.1. Game Concept**
*System Apocalypse - Urban Defender* is a single-player strategy game that blends LitRPG (Literary Role-Playing Game) progression with real-time strategy and hero management. Players are tasked with defending a modern city from a relentless monstrous invasion. The game begins at the moment of the "Awakening," a world-changing event where reality is integrated with game-like mechanics. The player manages a team of up to 10 emergent heroes, guiding their development and using their unique abilities to protect the city's dwindling population. The population is abstractly represented by residential buildings and civilian vehicles, creating a unique strategic layer where protecting infrastructure is paramount to survival.

**1.2. Genre**
LitRPG, Strategy, Hero Management

**1.3. Target Audience**
Fans of LitRPG and GameLit novels, strategy game enthusiasts, and players who enjoy hero collectors and defense-oriented games.

**1.4. Unique Selling Points**
*   **Abstracted Population:** Instead of managing individual citizens, players protect buildings and cars. This makes losses feel significant on a city-wide scale and streamlines macromanagement.
*   **LitRPG Core:** The game is built around beloved LitRPG tropes like system announcements, status screens, and tangible character progression with visible stats and skills.
*   **Card-Based Skill System:** Hero abilities are represented by cards, allowing for a deep and intuitive system of skill acquisition, combination, and enhancement.
*   **Urban Defense:** The setting of a modern city provides a unique backdrop for monster attacks, with heroes using magic to empower everyday cars and shield skyscrapers.

***

### 2. Core Gameplay Loop

The gameplay is structured around a cycle of Defense, Recovery, and Growth.

**2.1. The Awakening (Introduction)**
The game opens on a bustling cityscape with traffic flowing normally. Suddenly, a global countdown appears in the sky. As it hits zero, a world-shattering notification appears to all inhabitants:

> **[SYSTEM ANNOUNCEMENT: The Tutorial Phase has concluded. Commencing Integration.]**
> **[Welcome, User, to the System. Your world has now been integrated. Survive. Evolve. Prevail.]**

All cars screech to a halt. Chaos erupts as grotesque monsters begin to materialize, attacking buildings and infrastructure. The player is introduced to their first three heroes through a dialogue sequence. They are confused but quickly discover their new "Status Screens," a core element of LitRPG where characters can view their stats and abilities. This intro sequence serves as a tutorial, explaining the basic mechanics of hero skills while showcasing the overwhelming nature of the attack. By the end of this scripted event, 70% of the city is in ruins, the population has plummeted from 1000 to 300, and only 3 of the initial 10 heroes have survived.

**2.2. Defend and Rebuild Cycle**
*   **Defense Phase:** Monsters will attack the city in waves, targeting the remaining buildings. The player must use their heroes' abilities to support the city's defenders and defeat the monsters. The cars, driven by AI-controlled residents aligned with the player's Strikers and Vanguards, will automatically patrol the defended areas and engage monsters. The player's role is to support these automated defenders with powerful hero skills.
    *   **Tanks** (in cars) will automatically draw the aggro of monsters, pulling them away from buildings.
    *   **DPS** heroes (in cars) will automatically use ranged attacks to destroy the monsters.
    *   **Magic** heroes will cast shields on buildings and recharge the cars, as directed by the player.
*   **Recovery Phase:** After a wave is defeated, the player has a chance to repair and rebuild.
    *   Magic heroes use their abilities to repair damaged buildings and "un-wreck" cars.
    *   Repaired buildings allow the city's abstract population to grow.
*   **Growth Phase:** As the population recovers and heroes level up, the player can expand their protected zone, reclaim more of the city, and unlock new challenges and resources.

### 3. The Heroes

Heroes are the player's primary agents. They are represented by portraits in the UI and their skills are managed through a card-based system.

**3.1. Hero Learning Paths (Classes)**
Each hero must choose one of three distinct learning paths. This choice is permanent.

*   **The Aegis (Magic):** The backbone of the city's survival. They do not engage in direct combat but stay within shielded structures.
    *   **Core Resource:** Mana Points (MP), which regenerate over time based on their level and Intelligence stat.
    *   **Core Skills:**
        *   **Mana Battery:** Imbue a broken-down car with a magical power source, making it drivable for a Tank or DPS resident.
        *   **Aegis Shield:** Create a temporary, visible energy shield around a building or an allied car, absorbing a certain amount of damage.
        *   **Urban Mending:** Repair damaged buildings and cars, restoring their Health and functionality. This skill is crucial for increasing the population.

*   **The Striker (Ranged DPS):** The primary damage dealers, they operate from magically-powered cars.
    *   **Core Resource:** Health Points (HP), representing the durability of their car.
    *   **Core Skills:**
        *   **Mana Bolt:** A basic, single-target magic missile.
        *   **Arcane Artillery:** A powerful area-of-effect (AoE) attack that can hit multiple monsters.
        *   **Kinetic Shot:** A high-damage spell that can pierce through weaker enemies.

*   **The Vanguard (Tank):** The protectors of the city, driving reinforced cars designed to withstand heavy punishment.
    *   **Core Resource:** Health Points (HP), significantly higher than Strikers.
    *   **Core Skills:**
        *   **Taunt:** Forces all monsters in a radius to focus their attacks on the Vanguard's car for a short duration.
        *   **Reinforce Plating:** A self-buff that temporarily increases the car's armor and damage resistance.
        *   **Kinetic Ram:** A charging attack that damages and stuns a single target.

**3.2. Hero Progression and Skills**
Progression is central to the LitRPG experience. Heroes gain experience points (XP) from defeating monsters and repairing the city.

[[A detailed view of a Hero's "Status Screen" UI. It shows their portrait, name, level, XP bar, and core stats like [Strength], [Dexterity], [Intelligence], and [Stamina]. The screen has a clean, futuristic blue holographic look.]]

*   **The Card System:**
    Each skill is represented as a digital card. When a hero levels up, they may draw a new skill card or be offered an upgrade to an existing one.
    *   **Acquiring Cards:** New cards are gained on level-up or as rare drops from powerful monsters.
    *   **Stacking Cards:** Acquiring a duplicate of a skill card automatically "stacks" it, combining them to increase the skill's level and effectiveness. For example, stacking a second "Mana Bolt [Lv. 1]" card turns it into "Mana Bolt [Lv. 2]," increasing its damage and reducing its cooldown.
    *   **Skill Synergy:** Some advanced cards may require a hero to have specific other skills at a certain level, encouraging strategic development.

**Example Card:**

> **Card Name:** Aegis Shield [Lv. 3]
> **Type:** Active Skill - Magic (Aegis)
> **Mana Cost:** 75 MP
> **Description:** Projects a shimmering hexagonal shield around a target building. The shield has 500 Health and lasts for 30 seconds or until destroyed.
> **Stack Bonus:** +150 Shield Health per level.

[[A visual representation of the skill card management UI. A grid shows the hero's equipped skill cards. On the side, a new "Mana Bolt" card is shown dragging over an existing "Mana Bolt" card, with a glowing effect to indicate they are about to stack and level up.]]

### 4. The City and Population

The city is not just a map; it's a living entity representing the health of the population.

**4.1. Abstract Population Mechanics**
*   **Buildings as Population:** Each standing, undamaged residential building contributes to the total population count. When a building is attacked and its "health" reaches zero, it becomes a ruin, and the population decreases.
*   **Survivors and Repopulation:** When an Aegis hero uses "Urban Mending" on a ruin, it is restored. Survivors from other areas will then repopulate the building, increasing the population cap.
*   **Hero-Population Link:** Each hero is a leader for up to 100 residents. The total number of residents determines the number of heroes the player can field (1 hero per 100 residents, up to a max of 10 heroes at 1000 population). If a hero's associated population is wiped out, that hero has a high chance of dying permanently.

[[A top-down strategic view of a city block. Some buildings are pristine, some are smoking ruins, and one is currently being repaired by a golden light effect originating from a shielded building where an Aegis hero resides.]]

**4.2. Cars: The Agents on the Street**
*   **Representation of Force:** The active, fighting population is represented by cars. Magic residents (Aegis heroes and their followers) remain in buildings, while DPS and Tank residents drive the cars.
*   **Deployment:** One car can be deployed for every 10 residents aligned with a Striker or Vanguard hero. For example, a Vanguard hero representing 70 people can field up to 7 tanking cars.
*   **AI Control:** The player does not directly control the cars. Once deployed, these cars will patrol the secured streets and automatically engage any monsters that enter their range. The player's strategic input comes from using their heroes' powerful, overarching abilities to support the AI-driven cars, such as casting shields, healing them, or unleashing devastating area-of-effect attacks.
*   **Functionality:** Cars have their own HP bars. When a car's HP reaches zero, it becomes a wreck. The resident driving it is considered "dead," reducing the population by one. The car remains on the field as a wreck until an Aegis hero repairs it and replaces its "Mana Battery."

[[An action shot at street level. A heavily armored, glowing blue car (Vanguard) is ramming a shadowy beast, while several sleeker cars (Strikers) fire red bolts of energy from a distance. A visible health bar is floating above each car.]]

### 5. The Enemies

The monsters are a mysterious, otherworldly force intent on consuming the city.

**5.1. Monster Types**
*   **Crawlers:** Basic, numerous swarm-type monsters that focus on overwhelming buildings with sheer numbers.
*   **Goliaths:** Large, slow-moving brutes with high health. They are drawn to Vanguards but can level a building quickly if left unchecked.
*   **Spitters:** Ranged monsters that stay at a distance, launching corrosive projectiles at cars and shields.
*   **Breachers:** Specialized monsters that deal extra damage to Aegis shields, requiring focused fire from Strikers.

### 6. User Interface (UI)

The UI will be clean, modern, and non-intrusive, allowing the player to focus on the strategic situation while providing all necessary information clearly, following trends in modern strategy games.
**6.1. Main Game Screen**
*   **Top Bar:** Displays global resources: Current/Max Population, Number of Active Heroes, and threat level.
*   **Left Side:** Hero portraits. Clicking a portrait selects the hero. The portraits will flash if the hero's units are under attack.
*   **Bottom Center:** When a hero or unit is selected, this area shows their status (HP/MP), skills (cards), and available actions.
*   **Mini-Map:** A corner map showing the entire city, with color-coding for safe zones, ruined zones, and incoming enemy waves.

[[A mockup of the full game screen. It shows a city view with units fighting. The hero portraits are on the left, the minimap is in the bottom right, and a selected hero's skill cards are displayed along the bottom.]]

**6.2. Hero Management Screen**
Accessed by double-clicking a hero portrait, this screen is the hub for progression.
*   **Left Pane:** The hero's full Status Screen, showing stats, level, and a detailed list of passive abilities.
*   **Right Pane:** A larger view of their skill card deck. Here, players can review card details, see potential upgrade paths, and plan their hero's build.

[[A dedicated UI screen for Hero Management. On the left is the detailed holographic status sheet. On the right is an elegant display of the hero's skill cards, with subtle animations and glowing borders to indicate rarity or level.]]
